About the Book

What if Germany had won the Great War in 1918? A century later, a historian reopens the files and uncovers a disturbing truth. A few altered words—and the history of Europe, and of the world, takes a different course. From the trenches of the Somme to the corridors of Brussels, from the fall of Paris to the birth of Pan-Europe, Claire Moreau, a restless intellectual, moves through the century as the critical conscience of the new order. Through her, the novel probes the heart of the twentieth century: a pacified continent born under German hegemony and sustained by a fragile balance between order, progress, and the need for shared memory.

Europa is a political thriller, a historical reflection, a novel about the power of ideas, the price of peace, and the thin line that separates civilisation from ruin.
